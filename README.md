I build software around machine learning, language models, and data. I am most at home in the space between an interesting prototype and a system people can genuinely rely on.

Good software should feel calm: clear boundaries, useful defaults, and no unnecessary cleverness. That is the standard I try to bring to everything I make.

## What I am exploring

- **Reliable AI agents** — tools that can reason over real data, take useful actions, and show their work.
- **Retrieval and evaluation** — making language-model systems grounded, measurable, and easier to improve.
- **ML infrastructure** — the APIs, pipelines, and developer tooling that turn models into maintainable products.

My main tools are Python, FastAPI, PyTorch, Google Cloud, Docker, and Terraform. I also work with TypeScript and C++ when the problem calls for them. I care more about choosing a small, coherent stack than collecting technologies.

## A little more about me

I am pursuing an M.Sc. in Informatics and enjoy learning by building: starting with a question, reducing it to its essential parts, and leaving behind something another person can understand and reproduce.

Most of my professional work lives in private repositories. This profile is where I am building the public side of my work through focused projects, engineering notes, and useful experiments.

Right now, that means spending time on agentic analytics, production-minded RAG, and practical ML systems—from the first design decision to the final deployment.
